International Student Guide in St. Petersburg

A complete survival and lifestyle guide for international students living in Saint Petersburg, Russia — including housing, food, transportation, work opportunities, climate preparation, finances, student life and essential recommendations.

Explore Guide

Welcome to St. Petersburg

St. Petersburg is one of the most beautiful and student-friendly cities in Russia. Known for its historic architecture, museums, nightlife, canals and cultural atmosphere, the city attracts thousands of international students every year.

This guide helps new students navigate life in the city — from finding affordable groceries and good restaurants to discovering parks, entertainment spots and job opportunities.

Top Places to Visit

Palace Square

One of the most iconic places in St. Petersburg and home to the famous Winter Palace and Hermitage Museum.

Nevsky Prospekt

The city’s main avenue filled with cafes, stores, bookstores, restaurants and nightlife.

Peterhof Gardens

A breathtaking historical estate known for its fountains and palace architecture.

Food & Restaurants

Affordable Student Cafes

Explore local cafeterias and budget-friendly student restaurants around university areas.

Fast Food

International chains and local fast food spots are widely available throughout the city.

International Cuisine

African, Asian, Middle Eastern and European restaurants can be found around central districts.

Shopping & Essentials

Grocery Stores

Popular chains include Perekrestok, Lenta, Pyaterochka and Magnit for affordable groceries.

Clothing Stores

Visit Galeria Mall and other shopping centers for fashion, winter clothes and student essentials.

Electronics

Electronics stores such as DNS, M.Video and Eldorado offer laptops, phones and accessories.

Budget Shopping

Stores like Fix Price, Wildberries, OZON and second-hand shops are great for students trying to save money.

Student Work Opportunities

Language Tutoring

International students often work as English tutors or language conversation assistants.

Freelancing

Online freelance work in graphic design, programming, editing and social media management is common.

Restaurant & Delivery Jobs

Part-time jobs in cafes, restaurants and delivery services are available for students with flexible schedules.

Important Laws for International Students

Migration Registration

After arriving in Russia, international students must register at their place of residence. Most universities or dormitories help with this process, but students should always verify it personally.

Visa & Documents

Always monitor the validity of your passport, visa, migration card and registration documents. Expired documents can lead to fines or restrictions.

Medical Insurance

Medical insurance (DMS policy) is mandatory for most international students in Russia.

Official Employment

Full-time international students are generally allowed to work officially under Russian law if conditions are met, especially within the region of study.

Understanding the Climate

Winter

Winters can reach -15°C with strong wind and humidity. Students should invest in proper winter jackets, boots and thermal clothing.

Autumn & Spring

Rain and wind are very common. Waterproof shoes, umbrellas and layered clothing are highly recommended.

Summer

Summers are usually cool and pleasant, averaging between 18°C and 25°C.

Useful Apps for Students

Navigation

Yandex Maps, 2GIS and Yandex Go are extremely useful for navigation, taxis and deliveries.

Translation

Google Translate and DeepL help international students communicate more easily.

Study Apps

Notion, Quizlet and Duolingo can improve organization and language learning.

Best Banks for International Students

T-Bank

Known for its modern app, smooth transfers and student-friendly interface.

SberBank

Russia’s most widespread bank with ATMs almost everywhere.

Alfa Bank

Popular for cashback offers and modern mobile banking features.

Improving Your Russian Language Skills

Practice Daily

Speaking Russian for even 15–20 minutes daily greatly improves fluency and confidence.

Watch Russian Content

Russian YouTube videos, podcasts and TV series with subtitles help students improve listening skills.

Language Exchange

Apps like Tandem and HelloTalk help students communicate with native speakers.

Social Activities & Student Life

Student Communities

Join international student groups through Telegram, VK and university organizations.

Nightlife & Events

Explore concerts, cultural festivals, open mic nights and social gatherings around the city.

Parks & Recreation

Relax at parks such as Summer Garden and Krestovsky Island during weekends.
